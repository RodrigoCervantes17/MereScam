The 4 Universal Scam "Red Flags"

No matter how creative scammers get, almost every single scam follows these four exact patterns. Memorize these red flags!

1

They Pretended to be Someone Official

Scammers love playing roles. They will claim to be a government officer (IRS, Medicare, Social Security), your bank's fraud investigator, a support technician, or a loved one in an emergency. Always call back using a publicly listed official number.

2

They Say There's an "Urgent" Problem or Prize

They create extreme urgency so you don't have time to think or double-check. They might say you'll get arrested, your accounts will freeze, a virus is deleting your computer files, or you won a prize but must claim it immediately.

3

They Pressure You to Pay in Specific Ways

Scammers want money in untraceable ways. They will demand that you buy Gift Cards (Target, Apple, Google Play), use wire transfers (Western Union), or buy Cryptocurrency. No real business or agency will ever ask for gift cards.

4

They Ask You to Keep it a Secret

They will tell you not to tell anyone—especially your family, your bank teller, or the police. They know that if you talk to someone else, they will realize it's a scam. Always share the story with a friend first.

What to Do If You've Been Targeted

If you fell for a scam or shared sensitive info, don't feel ashamed. These are professional criminals who trick thousands of people daily. Take a deep breath, and take these exact actions right now:

1

Call Your Bank Instantly

If you sent money, gave your credit card number, or entered your banking portal, call the card issuer or bank immediately using the phone number listed on the physical back of your card. They can freeze cards and stop transactions.

2

Change Your Passwords

If you gave away a password or had a hacker lock you out, log in from a safe device, change your password, and enable Two-Factor Authentication if available.

3

Report It to the Authorities

Reporting scams helps the government track criminals and alert others. It takes less than 5 minutes to submit an anonymous online report.

Emergency Contacts

Official channels to call or report scams in the United States:

  • Federal Trade Commission (FTC) Visit reportfraud.ftc.gov
  • National Elder Fraud Hotline Call 1-833-FRAUD-11 (1-833-372-8311)
  • Identity Theft Protection identitytheft.gov
Remember: You are not alone. Family, friends, and support specialists are always ready to help. Just talk to them!
